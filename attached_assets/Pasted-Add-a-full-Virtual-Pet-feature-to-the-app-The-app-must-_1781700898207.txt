The app must still feel like an educational learning app first, but the virtual pet should make children excited to come back, learn, and look after something.

Allow existing profiles that do not yet have a pet to choose one the next time they open the app.

Pet button / navigation

Add a new main button called:

“My Pet”

This button takes the child to the virtual pet area.

Show it clearly on the dashboard, but keep the rest of the app educational.

The app should not become only a pet game. The pet should support learning.

Pet room

When clicking My Pet, open the pet’s bedroom/playroom.

The room should match the child’s selected theme.

If the child has a girly theme:

* Cute pastel bedroom
* Stars, hearts, flowers, soft colours

If the child has a boyish theme:

* Adventure room
* Space, dinosaurs, cars, robots, blue/green/orange colours

Neutral:

* Bright colourful playroom
* Animals, stars, balloons, rainbow, planets

The pet should not just sit still.

The pet should:

* Walk around the room
* Blink
* Smile
* Bounce
* Look around
* React when tapped
* Play with toys
* Sleep when tired
* Get excited when fed
* Splash during bath time
* Celebrate when the child completes learning

Add small animations:

* Tail wag
* Ear wiggle
* Jump
* Spin
* Happy hearts
* Sparkles
* Sleep bubbles
* Bath bubbles

Pet needs

The child should really look after the pet.

Add pet needs:

* Hunger
* Thirst
* Cleanliness
* Sleep/Energy
* Happiness
* Learning

Show these as cute status bars or icons.

Examples:

* Food bowl
* Water bottle
* Bath bubbles
* Bed/moon
* Heart
* Book/star

Needs should slowly go down over time.

Important:
Timers must continue while the app is closed.

Do this by saving timestamps, not running timers in the background.

Example:

* Save lastFedTime
* Save lastBathTime
* Save lastWaterTime
* Save lastSleepTime
* Save lastPlayTime
* When app opens, compare saved timestamps to current time
* Update pet needs based on time passed

Example timers:

* Water needed every 2 hours
* Bath needed every 3 hours
* Food needed every 4 hours
* Play needed every 3 hours
* Sleep/rest needed every 6 hours
* Learning boost once per day

Keep it positive. Do not make the pet sad in a harsh way.

Use friendly messages:

* “Your capybara missed you!”
* “Your puppy is ready for a bath!”
* “Your bunny is thirsty!”
* “Your dragon wants to learn with you!”
* “Your pet feels happy after learning!”

Pet growth system

The pet should have many growth stages, not just 1–3.

Create a proper growth journey.

Example stages:

1. Tiny baby
2. Baby
3. Curious baby
4. Toddler pet
5. Playful pet
6. Little learner
7. Growing pet
8. Junior pet
9. Clever pet
10. Confident pet
11. Big kid pet
12. Teen pet
13. Skilled pet
14. Wise pet
15. Fully grown pet

Each stage should:

* Slightly change the pet’s size/look
* Unlock something new
* Feel rewarding
* Require learning progress, not just time

Growth should depend on:

* Games completed
* Daily challenges completed
* Learning streaks
* Badges earned
* Pet care completed
* Accuracy in games

Example:

* Complete 10 games = grow to stage 2
* Complete 25 games = grow to stage 3
* Complete 50 questions = unlock accessory
* Complete 5 daily challenges = grow again
* Earn reading badge = unlock book accessory
* Earn maths badge = unlock star collar

Do not let children grow the pet only by tapping feed/play repeatedly.
Learning must be the main way the pet grows.

Changing pets

Allow the pet to be changed in Settings or Edit Child Profile.

Important warning:
Changing pet resets the current pet’s progress and accessories.

Show warning:
“Changing your pet will reset this pet’s growth, care progress, and accessories. Your learning scores, badges, and child profile will stay safe.”

If confirmed:

* Reset pet growth
* Reset pet accessories
* Reset pet care stats
* Choose new pet
* Keep educational progress, scores, badges, notes, and profile data

This helps existing app users add a pet even if they did not originally choose one.
