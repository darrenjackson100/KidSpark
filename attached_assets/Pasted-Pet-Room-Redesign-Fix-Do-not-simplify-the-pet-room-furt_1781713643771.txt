Pet Room Redesign Fix

Do not simplify the pet room further.

The last update removed too much.

Current problems:

* Food, Water, Clean, Energy, Fun and Learning bars are gone from the pet room.
* The room looks empty and lifeless.
* It looks like a basic prototype.
* The pet is sitting in an empty room with bowls on the floor.
* The room does not feel like a child's bedroom.
* The pet still does not feel alive.
* The room no longer gives enough information about how the pet is feeling.

---

Bring the status bars back

Add back:

* Food
* Water
* Cleanliness
* Energy
* Fun
* Learning

These must always be visible while viewing the pet.

Do not hide them.

The child should instantly know:

* Is my pet hungry?
* Is my pet thirsty?
* Is my pet tired?
* Does my pet want to play?
* Does my pet want to learn?

Make the bars visually attractive.

Examples:

* Food = bowl icon
* Water = water icon
* Clean = bubbles icon
* Energy = moon icon
* Fun = toy icon
* Learning = book icon

If Learning drops:

Pet may say:
"Can we do some learning together?"

If Fun drops:

Pet may say:
"I want to play!"

If Food drops:

Pet may say:
"I'm hungry!"

The speech should match the actual bars.

---

Stop making the room look empty

Current room looks like:

* Empty walls
* Empty floor
* Random bowl on floor
* Random pet

This is not a child's bedroom.

Create a proper room.

Include:

* Bed
* Pet bed
* Window with curtains
* Bookshelf
* Toy shelf
* Wall paintings
* Posters
* Plush toys
* Lamp
* Rug
* Small decorations
* Storage boxes

The room should feel warm and lived in.

---

Make the room look like a real bedroom

Do not use:

* Plain coloured walls
* Huge empty spaces
* Random floating objects

Use:

* Textures
* Depth
* Shadows
* Decorations
* Layered furniture

The room should look like somewhere a child would actually sleep and play.

---

Pet should feel alive

The pet still feels like a sticker.

Requirements:

* Blink
* Look around
* Breathe
* Smile
* Sit
* Lie down
* React when tapped
* Move slightly around room

If proper walking is difficult:

Do not fake it.

Instead:

* Make the pet mostly stay on the rug
* Use high quality idle animations
* Make it feel alive through movement

This is better than awkward sliding.

---

Pet speech bubble

Keep speech bubble above the pet.

Not at the top of the room.

Not on the pet's face.

Not overlapping furniture.

Speech bubble should track pet position.

---

Care buttons

Keep:

* Feed
* Water
* Bath
* Rest
* Play
* Learn

But make them look premium.

Consistent sizing.
Consistent spacing.

---

Important visual direction

The pet room should feel closer to:

* A premium children's virtual pet game
* A cosy bedroom
* A child looking after a pet companion

It should NOT feel like:

* A prototype
* A dashboard
* An empty room
* A pet sitting in a box

The pet must be the star of the room.

The room must support the pet, not replace it.

Do not remove the bars again.

Do not simplify the room further.

Improve quality rather than removing things.
