Add a full child profile editing system.

Adults must be able to edit a child’s:

* Name
* Age range
* Profile icon/avatar
* Notes

This is important because children may have birthdays and need to move into a new learning age group.

When the child’s age range is changed:

* Immediately update the games they can see.
* Only show games for the new age range.
* Keep old progress saved.
* Keep old scores saved.
* Separate progress by age range so nothing gets mixed together.
* Add a message saying:
  “Age range updated. [Child Name] is now learning with [Age Range] activities.”

* 3–4 Years

Add more educational sections:

Reading & Words
Make this age appropriate.

3–4 Years:

* Match picture to word
* Match letter sounds
* Find the first letter
* Simple alphabet games
* Tap the correct letter
* Match uppercase and lowercase letters
* Fill in missing letter with picture help
* Example: C _ T with a cat picture

5–6 Years:

* Fill in missing words
* Simple sentence completion
* Rhyming words
* Beginning and ending sounds
* Match word to picture
* Read short words
* Choose the correct word
* Example: “The dog can ___.” options: run, sun, red

7–8 Years:

* Fill in the correct word in a sentence
* Short reading comprehension
* Word meanings
* Spelling challenges
* Sentence ordering
* Nouns, verbs and adjectives
* Longer missing word questions
* Example: “The rabbit quickly ran into the ___.” options: garden, pencil, moon
